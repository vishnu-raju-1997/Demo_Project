Medical Emergency

Dog Bite

If bitten by a dog, wash the wound with soap and water and seek medical attention. Do not provoke the dog and try to remain calm.

Heart Attack

If experiencing symptoms of a heart attack (chest pain, shortness of breath), call emergency services immediately and chew aspirin if available.

Learn More

Burns

If burned, cool the affected area with cold running water for at least 10 minutes. Cover the burn with a clean, non-stick bandage.

Learn More

Choking

If someone is choking, perform the Heimlich maneuver by standing behind the person and applying upward pressure on the abdomen, just above the navel.

Learn More

Stroke

If someone shows signs of a stroke (facial drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty), call emergency services immediately.

Learn More

Fracture

If someone has a suspected fracture, immobilize the injured area and seek medical attention. Do not try to realign the bones.

Learn More

Allergic Reaction

If someone has a severe allergic reaction (difficulty breathing, swelling), use an epinephrine auto-injector if available and seek emergency medical help.

Learn More

Seizure

If someone is having a seizure, stay calm, protect them from injury, and time the seizure. Call for medical help if it lasts longer than 5 minutes.

Learn More

Poisoning

If someone is poisoned, call the poison control center immediately. Do not try to induce vomiting unless instructed by professionals.

Learn More

Drowning

If someone is drowning, call emergency services, and perform CPR if trained. Remove them from the water and begin rescue breathing.
